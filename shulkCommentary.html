[1] Prologue

To the commentators:


I have nothing but respect for you all and for the effort you put in in order to tell stories through the players and put on a good show for the viewers. You guys are in many ways the face of our community, providing analysis and excitement and really bringing events to life (especially high profile events). On top of that, for many viewers, you are one of the main sources of information on character specifics.


To that end, I wanted to take the time to arm you with the knowledge you need to make sure that you don’t get caught off guard when commentating for Shulk players. Shulk commentary has become a meme both within the Shulk community and in the Smash community at large. I’ve provided easy-to-sift-through summaries of all of Shulk’s nuances and advanced techniques. If you are not sure of something specific regarding Shulk’s kit, please use these notes to confirm. The important thing to avoid is spreading misinformation as fact, which has been so rampant when commentating Shulk sets up to this point.


If you have a Shulk player in your region, or if you know a region where Shulk commentary is notoriously lackluster, please send these notes to the region’s commentary team! I hope that by the end of Smash 4’s lifespan, we can finally stop fearing the creation of a second “Shulk Commentators Say the Darndest Things” video.


Orlando Fernandez

“Tremendo Dude”



Important Commentary Notes:


Please do not refer to Monado Arts as “Monados” or “____ Monado”

  • (ie: Jump Monado)

  • This is a pet peeve for many Shulk players, despite how commonplace this is.

    • Monado is the name of the sword, and saying “Monados” makes it sound like Shulk is switching between multiple swords.

  • Instead, refer to Monado Arts in the following ways if you don’t want any backlash from Shulk players:

    • “Art” or “Arts” (ie: Jump Art, switching Arts) [Preferred]

    • “Monado ____” (ie: Monado Jump) [How it's said in Xenoblade]

    • “Mode” (ie: Jump Mode) [How it's said in Smash]


[2] Shulk Basics


[2a] Monado Arts

Move Details:

  • Arts activate 3/4ths of a second after cycling.

  • Arts can be cycled/deactivated at any time Shulk isn’t in an animation, being grabbed, or in hitlag.

  • Arts can be cycled/deactivated during hitstun.


Monado Art Activation Pose

FAF: Frame 6

Intangible: Frame 1 - 14

  • Cancels all landing lag (aerials, Airdodge, and RCO) on activation.


See also:

  • MALLC (landing lag cancel)

  • Buffered Deactivation

  • Run Cancel (MARC)

  • Intangible Landing (MAIL)

  • MALS (ledge dash)

  • MA B-reverse


Monado Art Effects

Jump:

+ Increase Jump Height

+ Increase Air Speed

+ Increase Air Slash Height

= Increase Fall Speed

- Increase Damage Taken


Speed:

+ Increase Ground Speed

+ Increase Air Speed

= Decrease Jump Height

= Increase Friction [except when performing a Pivot action]

- Decrease Damage Dealt

Shield:

+ Decrease Damage Taken

+ Decrease Knockback Taken [affects Hitstun]

+ Increase Shield Health/Regen

= Decrease Jump Height

- Decrease Ground Speed

- Decrease Air Speed


Buster:

+ Increase Damage Dealt

= Decrease Knockback Dealt

- Increase Damage Taken


Smash:

+ Increase Knockback Dealt

- Increase Knockback Received

- Decrease Damage Dealt


Secondary effects

  • Damage Taken

    • Slightly affects Knockback Taken

  • Damage Dealt

    • Affects Damage Dealt vs Shields

    • Affects Shield Stun [Safety on shield]

  • Knockback Taken

    • Affects Hitstun Taken

    • Affects Weight Based Knockback Taken

  • Knockback Dealt

    • Affects Hitstun Dealt


[2b] Special Moves


Back Slash

Start-up: Frame 22

  • Deals bonus Damage and Knockback when hitting the opponent's back.

  • Displacement from the jump can be used to mix up opponents.

  • Effective against iframes thanks to lingering hitbox.

  • Effective as a shield breaker in Buster.

Air Slash

Start-up: Frame 10

  • Rising hit is Weight Based KB.

    • Early and Late hits almost always combos into second slash.

    • Middle hit is inconsistent (especially vs Fast Fallers and especially in Jump Art).

  • Second slash can be delayed to extend horizontal recovery or mix up ledge grab timing.

  • Can snap to ledge in two ways:

    • The start of the first slash (Cloud style, UpB next to ledge)

    • The start of the second slash (Space well, buffer the second slash).

      • Not using 2nd slash has the same snap timing as Cloud spacing upB.

  • Effective Out of Shield option due to its range and decent speed.

  • Can scoop opponents off the ledge.

  • Extremely effective edgeguard and anti-edgeguard tool.

  • Interrupting the second slash with a weak attack can trigger a laggy buffered input and cause Shulk to SD (unless Shulk inputs the second slash with the Grab button).


Vision

  • Unblockable counterattack.

  • Slows down any opponent who activates it with a direct attack.

    • Projectiles won't slow the user.

  • 3 different standard versions: Normal, Forward, and Air

  • Normal:

    • Shulk steps backwards before slashing forward. (Grounded)

  • Forward:

    • If activated on the ground and Shulk holds towards the direction of the attack, he will quickly dash in that direction and slash behind him.

    • Most commonly used, as it's the strongest and fastest.

  • Air:

    • Aerial version of Normal.

    • Vulnerable until frame 9, making it ineffective as a counter against multihits.

    • Slow still takes effect even if interrupted (acting like a pseudo Witch Time, jokingly referred to as “Shulk Time”)


See also:

  • Vision Slide

  • Shadow Eye

[3] Advanced Techniques


[3a] Monado Art Tech

MALLC - Monado Art Landing Lag Cancel

  • Refers to using the Monado Art Activation pose to interrupt landing lag, and optionally using the iframes on the pose to avoid counterattacks.

  • Cancels landing lag from aerial attacks, Airdodge, and RCO.

  • Most useful and versatile tech in Shulk’s kit.


Buffered Deactivation (MABD or BD)

  • Refers to buffering the deactivation of the Monado Arts in order to abuse the transition between an Art and Shulk’s Vanilla state.

  • When Shulk transitions out of an Art that affects his mobility, he will retain the mobility properties of that Art until he changes animation state.

  • Effects:

    • Jump: Shulk jumps (retaining Jump Height) and then deactivates, retaining changes to Fall Speed and Air Speed. However, if Shulk changes state during the jump (ie: attacking), the Fall Speed change will be cancelled out. If done early in the jump, Shulk will rise significantly higher than if he retained Jump Art.

      • Applications: Monado Purge (Progression 2), recovery/edgeguard mixups.

    • Speed: BD retains Speed’s change to ground speed, but does NOT retain friction. This allows Shulk to slide rather far with his grounded momentum.

      • Applications: Extending Dthrow strings, MonaDacus (Alt Name: Sad Bus / SABDJCUS)

    • Buster: No transition state, but turning off Buster mid-combo allows Shulk to turn Buster combos into potential kill confirms, thanks to the removal of Buster’s KB reduction.

      • Applications: Buster Nair > BD Fsmash (Alt Name: “The Good”)


MALS - Monado Art Ledge Snap

Alt name: Ledge Dash (MALD)

  • Refers to using the Monado Art Activation pose’s animation to clip onto the ledge from a ledge drop, while abusing the iframes from the activation pose to keep safe.

    • Comparable in effect to a Ledge Dash from Melee.

  • Practical method to perform is very stage dependent.

    • Legal stages it works on:

      • Battlefield

      • Smashville

      • Lylat Cruise

      • Suzaku Castle (Omega)

Intangible Landing (MAIL)

  • Refers to using the Monado Art Activation pose’s iframes in the air to land intangibly.

  • The pose also transitions smoothly from Air to Ground, allowing Shulk to skip Hard Landing lag entirely and perform grounded actions instantly when landing.


Run Cancel (MARC)

Formerly known as Dash Cancel (MADC)

  • Refers to using the Monado Art Activation pose to interrupt Shulk’s run, allowing him to perform any standing action or transition into a walk.


MA B-reverse / reverse B / wavebounce

  • Applies to the Monado Art Activation pose. Unique in that the input must be performed 3/4ths of a second after B is pressed to select the Art.


[3b] Vision Tech

Shadow Eye

Application of “Shadow iFrames”

  • Refers to a glitch that extends the iframes of Vision stance to cover the entire animation.

  • Performed by landing on frame 8 of Vision’s stance, skipping the flag in the move that turns off iframes by transitioning to grounded Vision.

  • Can also be applied to Air Vision’s counterattack if Shulk lands during the move and hits anything with the attack.

  • Named after an ability in Xenoblade.


Vision Slide

  • Refers to a glitch that allows Shulk to control his movement during his counterattack, allowing him to slide in any direction on the ground.

  • Impractical technique without Bidou.

  • Three variations:

    • Air to Ground:

      • Counter on the frame you land while in an interruptible animation.

      • Applies Air speed.

    • Pivot:

      • Counter without crouching during the turnaround animation.

      • Applies Walk speed.

    • Perfect Pivot

      • Counter without crouching during a Perfect Pivot’s turnaround animation.

      • Applies Run speed, but can only slide forward.


“Shulk Time”

  • Refers to exploiting the slowdown effect of Vision’s activation by purposely allowing Air Vision to be interrupted by a weak multi-hit attack during its vulnerable startup.

[3c] Misc Tech

Purge / Monado Purge

Alt name: 248 / The Ahmad (in SoCal)

  • Refers to Uthrow > Uair in Jump Art.

  • A 50/50 kill setup that works on almost every character in the game.

  • Five “Progressions” for different % windows:

    • Prog1: Buffered Uair

    • Prog2: BD Uair

    • Prog3: dj Uair

    • Prog4: Buffered Uair2

    • Prog5: BD Uair2

  • Named after an attack in Xenoblade.


Jab Walk / Combat Walk

  • Shulk can move forward a significant distance by alternating between Jab1 and Jab2.

  • Some characters can get carried across the stage if they are hit by Jab1 but not by Jab2.

[4] Common Misconceptions


[4a] General

  • Shulk can snap to the ledge with Air Slash (though it does requires some finesse).

    • Method 1: UpB next to the ledge (Cloud style snap).

    • Method 2: Space Air Slash properly and buffer the second slash.

  • Shulk does not “lose to shield”.

    • Jump Art’s momentum and Buster Art’s damage make Shulk’s aerials safe on shield.

    • MALLC makes Shulk’s attacks safe on shield.

    • Shulk can get a lot of reward off of grabs (damage, 50/50 kill setups, kill throws).


[4b] Monado Arts

Jump:

  • Does not make Shulk lighter.

    • Jump Art increases Fall Speed, which generally makes Shulk die later off the top and earlier off the side.

Shield:

  • Does not make Vision counter stronger.

    • Shulk players use Vision more in Shield Art for 2 reasons:

    • Lower Hitstun allows Shulk to fall out of combos and counter.

    • Vision is less risky thanks to Shield Art’s reduction of KB and damage.

  • Does not necessarily make Shulk “combo food”.

    • Shield Art reduces damage taken as well as hitstun. Although Shulk is easier to juggle, he can escape from light combo attacks at lower %s relatively easily, and the damage reduction means Shulk takes less % overall even if he gets juggled.

  • Does not make Shulk “heavier”.

    • Monado Art KB changes are multipliers that affect all KB, meaning Weight Based KB is also affected.

Buster:

  • Is not as risky as it seems.

    • Shulk deals 40% more damage per hit while only taking 12% more damage per hit.

    • Jump Art is actually riskier when it comes to this, as Shulk takes 22% more damage per hit.

    • Buster Art makes Shulk’s attacks safer on shield, which can make interactions less risky.

Smash:

  • Does not make Shulk “lighter”.

    • Monado Art KB changes are multipliers that affect all KB, meaning Weight Based KB is also affected.
